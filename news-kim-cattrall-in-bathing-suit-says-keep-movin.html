Kim Cattrall is an icon, best known as the siren Samantha Jones on Sex and The City. Now, into her 60's, Cattrall is still looking amazing. Prepping for a part on Peacock's reboot of Queer as Folk, Cattrall posed in a brown one-piece swimsuit on Instagram. She captioned the post, "Nothing to see here. Keep movin'!" How does she stay so fit? Cattrall revealed some of her wellness tips to People. She says that the time she grew up in, is one of the reasons she's in such great shape now. "This generation of women that I'm in, we have been brought up with Jane Fonda workout tapes and fresh air is good for you and exercise every day and drink eight cups of water," she says. "It's paid off at this time of my life.'df44d9eab23ea271ddde7545ae2c09ec

Cattrall tells People that when it comes to wellness, she does it for herself, not to look attractive to a man. "I don't do it for a guy. I do it for me. It's about taking care of yourself." "No matter your age, weight or athletic ability, aerobic activity is good for you. Aerobic activity has many health benefits. As your body adapts to regular aerobic exercise, you'll get stronger and fitter," says the Mayo Clinic. "Combined with a healthy diet, aerobic exercise helps you lose weight and keep it off."

Cattrall revealed her favorite workouts to Woman & Home Magazine. She says that she chooses exercises that don't strain her body, since she's dealt with injuries before. "I've had injuries in the past, so I also do 'physio yoga' – a combination of physio and yoga so I'm constantly trying to rehabilitate my body. The older I get, the less jarring I want my exercise to be and I find that a long walk is equally as helpful and satisfying as a three-mile jog.

For Cattrall, wellness is all about balance. She revealed her approach to dieting and wellness to Woman & Home, saying, I joke that I've been on a diet since 1974, which is basically true. I like to eat, and my body type is not naturally this thin – especially at this age. So I do watch what I eat and drink, but I'm not obsessive – it's just a way of life. So I don't have dessert after every meal – I just can't do it. I have a big appetite and I like to eat, and staying on top of that is about knowing myself and saying, 'I can eat that today, but tomorrow I'm not going to.' And I'm always aware from gaining and losing weight for parts, that the time in the gym trying to lose extra weight is really hard work! I always have that in my mind."

When it comes to skincare, Cattrall tells Woman & Home that she takes a quality over quantity approach. She says that she does things that help her skin, but doesn't have an intense routine. "I've got a very good dermatologist because I have very sensitive skin. I like to exfoliate and I wear make-up that has sun protection, but other than that it's a very lax regime. I wear a very minimal amount of make up in daily life because I like my skin to breathe."
