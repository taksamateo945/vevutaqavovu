In What Episode Does Ace Die in One Piece?

Ace’s story in One Piece comes to a tragic end in Episode 483 when he is captured by the Marines and sentenced to be executed for his association with the Pirate King.

Ace is a major character in the popular Japanese anime, One Piece created by Eiichiro Oda. He joined Monkey D. Luffy, whose life took a new turn when his body gains the properties of rubber after unintentionally eating a Devil Fruit. With his diverse crew of pirates, named the Straw Hat Pirates, Luffy sets out on a journey to find the world’s ultimate treasure, known as “One Piece,” and he ultimately becomes the King of the Pirates.

Does Ace Die in One Piece and In What Episode?

Ace dies in Episode 483 of the One Piece anime series. Ace’s death is a significant event in the storyline, and it occurs during the Marineford Arc and this was around a battle between the Marines and the Whitebeard Pirates, who are trying to rescue Ace from being executed.

Ace was previously captured by the Marines for his past affiliation with the infamous pirate Gol D. Roger, and the Marines ordered his execution as a means of deterring piracy.

Ace dies in Luffy’s arms because of the injuries he sustained during his fight with Akainu. The loss of Ace deeply affects Luffy, who had grown up with Ace as a brother and had always looked up to him. Luffy’s grief over Ace’s death serves as a pivotal moment in the series, as it drives him to become stronger and more determined to achieve his goals.

Who Killed Ace in One Piece?

Ace, also known as Portgas D. Ace, died in the Marineford War arc of One Piece. During the war, Ace was captured by the Marines and was sentenced to be executed by Admiral Akainu. Luffy and the rest of the Whitebeard Pirates launched a rescue mission to save Ace, but they were confronted by the Marines and the Seven Warlords of the Sea.

In a fierce battle, Ace was able to break free from his execution platform with the help of Luffy and the Whitebeard Pirates. However, Ace’s rescue was short-lived as Akainu continued to pursue him and attacked him with his magma powers. Ace shielded Luffy from the attack, which resulted in him receiving fatal wounds.

Ace was also able to have a final conversation with Luffy and thanked him for everything he had done for him. He revealed that he was the son of the Pirate King, Gol D. Roger, and that he was proud to have lived his life as a pirate. Ace passed away in Luffy’s arms, causing immense grief and sorrow to those who knew him.

How Significant was Ace’s Death in the Series?

The death of Ace in One Piece was a significant moment in the series, as it marked the end of a major storyline and had a profound impact on the main character, Monkey D. Luffy.
Ace was the adopted brother of Luffy a powerful pirate in his own right, known for his strength and his affiliation with the Whitebeard Pirates.

Ace’s death represented a turning point in the series, as it showed that even the strongest and most beloved characters could die. It also highlighted the brutal and unforgiving nature of the world of One Piece, where power and strength often determine one’s fate.

Ace’s death also had a profound impact on Luffy’s character development. It fuelled his determination to become stronger and protect his friends, and it marked the beginning of his quest to become the Pirate King and create a world where his friends could live freely without fear of persecution.
