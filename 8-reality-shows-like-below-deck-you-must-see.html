8 Reality Shows Like Below Deck You Must See

August 12, 2024
blog

The Bravo reality series “Below Deck” has amassed a sizable fan base over time. It follows the lives of employees who work atop a multi-million dollar luxury yacht. The reality show is comparable to any other workplace due to the unforeseen drama and inescapable disagreements between the captain, stewardesses, and deckhands. The series debuted in 2013, and it has since aired for ten seasons, growing in popularity. If the crew’s routine operations keep viewers interested, it’s the drama surrounding the disparate staff members’ methods that has kept viewers hooked for ten seasons.

Since its debut, “Below Deck” has served as the model for a number of spin-offs that highlight the operational overload that occurs in the background of various businesses. ‘Below Deck’ offers structure and excitement for viewers who prefer to see things from the other perspective. But we have you covered if you’re caught up and want to watch more programmes like “Below Deck.” We have compiled a list of reality television programmes that are comparable to “Below Deck” that will successfully combine drama and high tension. Several of these reality programmes, including “Below Deck,” are available to stream on Netflix, Hulu, or Amazon Prime.

Camp Getaway (2020)

This 2020 Bravo reality programme follows David Schreiber, the owner of Camp Getaway, and Claire Sorrels, the camp director, as they manage the high expectations of their visitors alongside seven inexperienced senior coordinators. While at an adult summer camp in Kent, Connecticut, “Camp Getaway” chronicles the lives of the new recruits as they deal with the strain and demands of their visitors, in contrast to the camp that many of us are used to. If staff turmoil in “Below Deck” captured your attention, “Camp Getaway” will be the ideal binge-watch reality show to switch to next.

Deadliest Catch: Bloodline (2020-)

Even though “Deadliest Catch: Bloodline” lacks the opulence and glamour of a million-dollar yacht, it makes up for it with stunning seaports nestled on an endless sea of blue. One is readily drawn to the ocean’s beauty, but watching fisherman catch some of the deadliest fish in choppy waters also makes one feel exhilarated.

Josh Harris, who stars in the programme, uses his father Phil Harris’s maps to navigate the water while fishing off the coast of Hawaii. Casey McManus and Jeff Silva, Josh Harris’ business partners, are also featured in the reality television programme on Discovery Channel. The three follow scribbles on Hawaiian fishing charts as they search for tuna, swordfish, barracuda, and other species. Fans of “Below Deck” will recognise the show’s strong connection to the ocean and be attracted to it naturally.

First Class (2022)

You should watch “First Class,” a Spanish Netflix reality series that follows the lavish lifestyles of friends from Barcelona, if unmatched luxury and demanding guests are your thing. The fun appears to never end as you travel to exotic areas in Europe with unmatched luxury and attend party after party.

While seeing a bunch of pals live the high life and party all day may seem monotonous, it gives viewers a glimpse into the extravagant way of life of complete strangers. Viewers are enticed to travel Europe’s cobblestone streets by the reality show’s inclusion of the vibrant splendour of Barcelona. As a result, “First Class” is the show to watch next if “Below Deck’s” exotic setting and multi-million dollar set intrigued you.

Selling Sunset (2019-)

The seven most successful female realtors in Los Angeles are the subjects of this Netflix reality television programme. The dynamics of the characters are revealed as they compete to be the finest in Los Angeles’ competitive market while working for the same agency in the Hollywood Hills and Sunset Strip. The television show provides insight into opulent residences in West Hollywood while providing constant entertainment and drama. This programme is ideal for “Below Deck” lovers who want to see luxury at its finest.

The Cruise (2016-2019)

This ITV classic explores the logistical difficulties encountered as crew members and visitors go to Alaska on a 142k-ton, 1082-foot-long ship. The British television show, which originally aired in 2016, follows the lives of passengers and crew members as they take a Mediterranean trip on board the Regal Princess. This series provides the kind of interaction and dynamics that make “Below Deck” so intriguing to “Below Deck” fans. Hence, if you like watching “Below Deck,” this programme ought to be your next pick.

Timber Creek Lodge (2016-2017)

Another programme that emphasises luxury and the stories that go with it. Jamie Murphy, Katy Ann Boyd, Nikita Williams, Jenna Gillund, Blake Dubler, and other staff members are featured in the show. The personnel, who were hired to manage a ski lodge close to Whistler, British Columbia, find themselves at odds over issues involving lodging, dining, and mountain hosting.

Similar to “Below Deck,” “Timber Creek Lodge” depicts the ups and downs of crew members as they deal with interpersonal difficulties and guest expectations. Fans of “Below Deck,” another Bravo series, will be drawn not only to the remote, breathtaking nature of British Columbia but also to the interplay between the cast members.

Unanchored (2018)

The brief series chronicles the lives of young, accomplished professionals as they set out on an exciting journey to the Bahamas to take part in a prestigious floating festival alongside 30 ships. While brief, this compelling series explores the mysterious lives of friends who quit their occupations to immerse themselves in Bahamas culture and custom.

Unachored on Bravo follows the lives of friends with Captain Jack Royds as they set sail to create life-long memories and new adventures, instead of the inevitable drama that comes with typical reality television series. This show will give viewers of “Below Deck” who find the yachting sector fascinating a close-up look at related yachting festivals.

Vanderpump Rules (2013-)

Maverick Lisa Vanderpump and her family’s efforts to open and run SUR Restaurant & Lounge in West Hollywood, Beverly Hills, are tracked. This Bravo series, which debuted in 2013, explores the difficulties the family faces as they set out to open a hip restaurant in Hollywood. For viewers who enjoyed seeing how things are done behind the scenes in “Below Deck,” “Vanderpump Rules” also follows Lisa Vanderpump and the staff’s highs and lows. The series is the ideal mixture of drama, making it the ideal show to watch following “Below Deck,” even though some people seek fame and others seek critical acclaim.
